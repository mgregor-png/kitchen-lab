# Crispy Rice & Poached Chicken Salad

Serves 2

## Method

1. Rinse the chicken breasts under cold water. Add the chicken breasts to a saucepan and cover with cold water. Add salt and set over a medium heat. Once the water starts to steam, turn the heat down low — it should be just under a simmer. Poach for 10 mins, then remove from the water and set aside to rest.

2. To make the dressing, mix the peanut butter, chilli crisp, soy sauce and sherry vinegar in a small bowl. Whisk until combined. If your peanut butter is thick, loosen the dressing with a few tablespoons of water until it’s pourable. Season to taste with salt.

3. To crisp the rice, heat the sesame oil in a non-stick frying pan over a medium-high heat. Once hot, add the rice and press it down into the pan. Cook undisturbed for 5 mins, then flip and cook for another 3 mins until golden and crisp. Transfer to a plate and leave to cool, then break into chunks.

4. Smash the cucumber lightly with the flat side of a knife, then roughly chop into bite-sized chunks. Cut the carrot into matchsticks (or grate it). Finely slice the spring onions, separating the whites and greens if you like.

5. Slice the rested chicken into thin strips.

6. Toss the chicken, crispy rice, carrots, cucumber, beansprouts and spring onion whites with the dressing until well coated.

7. Divide between bowls, then finish with coriander leaves, spring onion greens and a sprinkle of togarashi, if using.

## Ingredients

### Chicken
- 300g Chicken Breast
- 1tbsp Salt
- 200g Cooked Short Grain Rice
- 100g Cucumber
- 100g Carrot
- 60g Beansprouts
- 3 Spring Onion
- 1tbsp Crispy Chilli Oil
- 2tbsp Soy Sauce

### Rice
- 2tsp Sesame Oil

### Sauce
- 1tbsp Peanut Butter
- 2tbsp Sherry Vinegar

### To Serve
- 15g Fresh Coriander
- 1tbsp Shichimi Togarashi
