# Shakshuka

## Method
1. Heat the olive oil in a large frying pan over a medium heat and add the pilpelchuma or harissa, tomato puree, peppers, garlic, cumin and 3/4 of a teaspoon of salt. Stir and cook on a moderate heat for about 8 minutes to allow the peppers to soften. Add the tomatoes, bring to a gentle simmer and cook for a further 10 minutes until you have quite a thick sauce. Taste for seasoning.

2. Make eight little dips in the sauce. Gently break the eggs and carefully pour each into its own dip. Use a fork to swirl the egg whites a little bit with the sauce, taking care not to break the yolks. Simmer gently for 8-10 minutes, until the egg whites are set but the yolks are still runny (you can cover the pan with a lid if you wish to hasten the process). Remove from the heat, leave for a couple of minutes to settle, then spoon into individual plates and serve with the labneh or yoghurt.

## Ingredients
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tbsp Pilpelchuma or harissa paste
- 2 tsp tomato puree
- 2 large red peppers, cut into 0.5cm dice (300g in total)
- 4 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 5 large, very ripe tomatoes, chopped (800g in total); tinned are also fine
- 6 medium free-range eggs
- 120g Labneh or thick yoghurt
- salt
