# Lamb chops with pilpelchuma oil, tahini yoghurt and herb salad

## Method
1. Preheat the oven to 180c.

2. Start by making the pilpelchuma oil. Trim the top ⅛ off the garlic heads to reveal the cloves. Place the heads onto a piece of foil and season with a pinch of salt and a tablespoon of oil. Tightly wrap them and roast for 40 minutes, until nicely softened and golden. Set aside to cool completely. Once cool, squish them to release the roasted cloves from their papery skins.

3. Meanwhile, place the chipotles in a dry frying pan over a medium high heat and toast for 30 seconds on each side until fragrant and waxy. Put into a heatproof bowl and cover with enough boiling water to fully submerge. Weigh the chillies down with a small saucer and leave to rehydrate for 20 minutes.

4. Add the cumin, caraway and sesame seeds to the frying pan and toast for 3 minutes until golden and fragrant. Transfer to a pestle and mortar with 1/4 teaspoon of salt and coarsely crush. Remove the chillies from the water, roughly chop and add to the pestle and mortar along with the roasted garlic, aleppo, paprika. Work the ingredients into a loose paste, then mix in the butter and the remaining oil. The final product will be split and textured. Remove and place in a bowl, keeping it at room temperature.

5. In a large bowl, mix two tablespoons of the butter with the lamb chops and 1/4 teaspoon of salt and rub well. Leave to marinate for at least 30 minutes (If you are getting ahead of time, place it in the fridge for up to 48 hours and remove it 30 minutes ahead of cooking).

6. In a bowl, mix the yoghurt, tahini, one tablespoon of lemon juice and a pinch of salt until it&amp;#39;s fully incorporated. Place in the fridge until ready to serve.

7. Put the skillet pan over a medium-high heat. As the pan starts to heat up, use a pair of tongs to press the chops fat side down onto the pan and gently render the fat out for 3 minutes until it starts to crisp and caramelise. Remove from the pan and turn the heat to high. Grill the lamb chops for 3 minutes on each side until nicely charred. (This will take the chops to medium, so add more or less time depending on how you like them). Place on a tray to rest for 5 minutes. Melt one more tablespoon of the butter in the pan and pour over the lamb.

8. Meanwhile, in a small bowl, tumble the remaining herb salad ingredients with ⅛ teaspoon of salt and the remaining lemon juice.

9. To serve, remove the yoghurt from the fridge and spread over a large plate. Add the lamb chops and pour over their resting juices, placing the herb salad alongside.

## Ingredients
- 4 lamb chops, preferably bone in (400g)
- 3 small heads of garlic
- 45ml olive oil
- 2 dried whole chipotle chillies, stems and seeds removed
- 1 tsp cumin seeds
- 1/2 tsp caraway seeds
- 1 tbsp sesame seeds
- 1/2 tsp  aleppo chilli
- 1 tsp paprika
- 60g butter at room temperature
- 150g greek yoghurt
- 50g  tahini
- 11/2 tbsp lemon juice
- 2 tbsp mint, roughly chopped
- 2 tbsp parsley, roughly chopped
- 1 tbsp dill, roughly chopped
- 2 lebanese cucumbers (130g), peeled, deseeded and cut into cubes
- 50g grapes, sliced in half
- 1 shallot (40g), peeled and thinly sliced into rounds
- 1/4 tsp  sumac
- salt and black pepper
