# One-Pot Thai Green Chicken Curry

Serves 2

## Method

1. Slice the chicken thighs into bite-sized pieces. Trim the baby corn and mange tout. Finely slice the red chilli. Pick some coriander leaves for garnish and cut the lime into wedges.

2. Set a large saucepan over a medium heat. Pour in half the coconut milk and cook for 5 mins until the fat starts to split and it smells toasty.

3. Add the Thai Green Curry Paste and stir for another 5 mins until darker and super fragrant. Tip in the chicken and cook for 3–4 mins until just sealed.

4. Sprinkle in the sugar and let it bubble and caramelise for 2 mins. Pour in half the fish sauce and scrape up any sticky bits.

5. Add the rest of the coconut milk and the chicken stock. Bring to a gentle simmer and cook for 15 mins until slightly thickened and the chicken’s cooked through.

6. Chuck in the baby corn and mange tout for the final 2 mins. Stir through most of the coriander and the remaining fish sauce. Taste and season.

7. Serve over cooked rice and top with fresh chilli, crispy shallots, coriander leaves and a wedge of lime.

## Ingredients

### Chicken
- 70g Thai Green Curry Paste
- 400ml Coconut Milk
- 200ml Chicken Stock
- 500g Boneless Skinless Chicken Thigh
- 175g Baby Corn
- 30g Fresh Coriander
- 300g Cooked Rice (to serve)
- 1 Lime (to serve)
- 1 Red Chilli (to serve)
- Fresh Coriander (to serve)

### Rice
- Crispy Shallot (to serve)

### Sauce
- 1tbsp Sugar
- 3tbsp Fish Sauce

### Other
- 2tbsp Vegetable Oil
- 150g Mangetout
- Salt
- Black Pepper
