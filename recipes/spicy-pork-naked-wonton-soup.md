# Spicy Pork & Naked Wonton Soup

Serves 2

## Method

1. Rinse the rice and add it to a small saucepan with the water. Bring to the boil, then reduce to a low heat, cover and cook for 12 mins until tender. Turn off the heat and leave to steam while you get on with everything else.

2. Meanwhile, finely grate the garlic and ginger, and finely slice the spring onions, keeping half back for the broth step and some greens to serve. In a bowl, mix the pork mince with half the spring onions, the garlic, ginger, soy sauce, sesame oil and white pepper until well combined. Then roll into small meatballs. 

3. Heat a large frying pan over a medium-high heat. Add the mince mixture and cook for 6–8 mins, breaking it up with a spoon, until craggy and caramelised in places.

4. Pour in the chicken stock, soy sauce, white pepper and chicken powder. Bring to a simmer and cook for 3–4 mins so the broth picks up all the flavour from the pork. Add the pak choi halved and wonton wrappers (if using), and simmer for 2–3 mins until just tender.

5. Divide the rice between bowls. Spoon over the pork, pak choi and plenty of broth. Finish with the remaining spring onion greens, a drizzle of chilli oil and a scatter of sesame seeds.

## Ingredients

- 400g 5% Fat Pork Mince
- 4 Spring Onion
- 2 Garlic Clove
- 10g Fresh Ginger
- 1tbsp Light Soy Sauce
- 1tsp Sesame Oil
- .5tsp Ground White Pepper

- 600ml Chicken Stock
- 1tbsp Light Soy Sauce
- .5tsp Ground White Pepper
- 1tbsp Chicken Powder
- 2 Baby Pak Choi
- 5 Wonton Wrapper (optional)

- 120g Jasmine Rice
- 240ml Water

- 1tsp Crispy Chilli Oil
- 1tsp Toasted Sesame Seeds
