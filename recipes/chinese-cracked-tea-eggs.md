# Chinese Cracked Tea Eggs

Serves 6

## Method

1. Bring a saucepan of salted water to the boil. Gently lower in the eggs and boil for 6 mins. Transfer straight into a bowl of iced water to stop the cooking.

2. While the eggs are cooling, make the marinade. In a saucepan, combine the soy sauces, tea bags, five spice, star anise, cinnamon stick, sugar and water. Bring to the boil, then let it cool completely.

3. Once the eggs are cool, drain and roll each one around the empty saucepan to gently crack the shells all over, you want spiderweb cracks, not full-on carnage. If you don't trust yourself to be gentle, try gently tapping the egg with the back of a spoon.

4. Pop the cracked eggs into a clean jar or container, pour over the cooled marinade and make sure they’re fully submerged. Cover with a piece of kitchen paper and pop the lid on.

5. Chill in the fridge for at least 12 hrs, ideally 24, to build flavour and get that deep marbling. 
These eggs will last for up to 4 days in the fridge, the flavour gets stronger each day.

6. When ready to serve, peel and halve. Then crack over some black pepper and serve with whatever you fancy, salads, noodles - it all works!

## Ingredients

### Eggs
- 6 Egg
- 50g Soy Sauce
- 150g Dark Soy Sauce
- 3Tea Bag Black Tea
- 1tsp Chinese Five-Spice
- 1 Star Anise
- 1 Cinnamon Stick
- 50g Sugar
- 250ml Water
- Salt

### Noodles
- Black Pepper
