# Brothy Red Curry Noodles

Serves 2

## Method

1. Add the rice noodles to a heatproof bowl and cover with boiling water. Let them sit for 4 mins until softened, then drain and rinse under cold water. Set aside.

2. Dice the tofu into bite-sized cubes. Halve any large mushrooms and trim the broccoli into bite-sized florets.

3. Add a third of the coconut milk to a large, heavy-bottomed saucepan set over a medium heat. Let it cook for 4–5 mins until the fat begins to separate from the milk.

4. Stir in the red curry paste and cook for 4–5 mins, stirring regularly, until fragrant. Add the sugar and cook for another 2 mins. Stir through half of the fish sauce.

5. Pour in the remaining coconut milk along with water. Bring to a simmer.

6. Add the mushrooms and tofu and cook for 2 mins. Add the broccoli and prawns and simmer for a final 3 mins until the prawns are cooked through.

7. Squeeze in half of the lime and season the broth with the remaining fish sauce. Taste and adjust with more salt or lime juice, if needed.

8. Divide the noodles between two bowls and ladle over the brothy curry. Finish with lime wedges and a handful of chopped coriander.

## Ingredients

- 100g Dried Rice Noodles

- 300g Light Coconut Milk
- 150ml Water
- 30g Thai Red Curry Paste
- 10g White Sugar
- 2tbsp Fish Sauce
- 300g Raw King Prawn
- 50g Firm Tofu
- 150g Baby Button Mushroom
- 100g Tenderstem Broccoli
- 1 Lime
- 15g Fresh Coriander
- Salt
