# Braised lamb with maftoul and chickpeas

## Method
1. In a large bowl, mix the lamb chops with a tablespoon of the olive

2. oil, a teaspoon of salt and half a teaspoon of pepper, and rub

3. everything together well. Place a medium stockpot on high heat, add the

4. chops and sear for two minutes on each side, until golden-brown. Add the

5. quartered onion, carrots and celery, pour in a litre and a half of cold

6. water and bring to a boil. Lower the heat, skim the surface and add the

7. parsley, cinnamon, allspice, peppercorns and bay leaves. Leave to

8. simmer for an hour and a half, skimming occasionally and topping up the

9. water if it gets low, until the lamb is tender.

10. Meanwhile, heat the remaining oil in a medium frying pan. Add the

11. sliced onion and cook for 10-15 minutes, stirring often, until

12. caramelised and brown. Remove from the heat and set aside.

13. When the chops are soft, remove them from the stock and set aside.

14. Strain the stock and measure out 900ml (or 700ml if using couscous

15. instead of maftoul); reserve the carrots to serve with the lamb, if you

16. wish. Top up with water if necessary. Return 700ml of the liquid to the

17. pot (500ml if using couscous), bring to a boil, add the maftoul

18. and cook on a low to medium heat, stirring often, until al dente -

19. about 10 minutes. (If using couscous, pour it in, cover the pan, remove

20. from the heat and set aside for five minutes.) Stir the chickpeas and

21. cooked onion through the maftoul, add a quarter-teaspoon of salt and

22. warm through. Remove from the heat, cover with a lid and keep somewhere

23. warm.

24. Just before serving, reheat the last of the lamb stock. Add the lamb

25. and gently stir until warmed through. Pile the warm maftoul on a large

26. serving platter, put the lamb chops on top, spoon over some of the hot

27. stock and the yoghurt (you may need to loosen it up first by whisking it

28. a little), sprinkle with the remaining parsley and serve.

## Ingredients
- 8 lamb chops, on the bone
- 2 tbsp olive oil 
- Salt and black pepper 
- 3 medium onions, one peeled and quartered, two peeled and cut into 0.5cm slices
- 2 large carrots, peeled and cut into 3cm pieces
- 2 sticks celery, cut into 3cm pieces
- 40g parsley leaves, plus 1 tbsp extra, chopped, for garnish
- 2 cinnamon sticks
- 20 whole allspice berries
- 10 black peppercorns
- 2 bay leaves
- 300g maftoul or couscous
- 200g cooked chickpeas 
- 150g Greek yoghurt
